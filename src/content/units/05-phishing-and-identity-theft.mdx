## Phishing

**Phishing is a form of social engineering where an attacker impersonates a trusted entity (a bank, government, or brand) to trick victims into revealing sensitive information** — passwords, OTPs, PINs, or card details. It works by creating **fear, urgency, or curiosity** ("your account will be blocked", "claim your reward").

### Phishing methods

- **Email phishing** — fake emails that look like a bank, office, or social-media site.
- **Spear phishing** — a *targeted* attack on a specific person/organisation, using details gathered from social media to look authentic.
- **Whaling** — spear phishing aimed at **high-profile executives** (CEO, CFO) to steal secrets or authorise large transfers.
- **Vishing (voice)** — phone calls pretending to be bank/support staff.
- **Smishing (SMS)** — fraudulent texts with fake links or urgent warnings.
- **Website / clone phishing** — fake look-alike sites, or a real message copied and re-sent with a malicious link.

<Callout type="danger">
**Real example (whaling):** an accountant gets an urgent email that looks like it's from the CEO demanding an immediate wire transfer to a vendor. They comply — only to find the address was subtly **spoofed**.
</Callout>

## Identity theft (ID theft)

**Identity theft is illegally acquiring and using another person's personal identifying information (PII)** — name, date of birth, Aadhaar, passport, bank, or login details — usually for financial fraud.

### The identity-theft lifecycle

1. **Information acquisition** — gather PII via data breaches, phishing, dumpster diving, or buying stolen profiles on the dark web.
2. **Exploitation (the fraud)** — open fraudulent accounts, take loans, claim tax refunds, or run up medical bills in the victim's name.
3. **Consequences** — credit damage, legal trouble, and a long, costly process for the victim to clear their name.

### Types

- **Financial** — using data to take money or open accounts.
- **Medical** — using someone's identity to obtain medical services.
- **Account takeover** — seizing email, banking, or social accounts.
- **Fake / synthetic identity** — building new fake profiles or documents.

<Callout type="danger">
**Real example (medical ID theft):** a criminal steals patient insurance records from a breach and uses a victim's policy for expensive procedures — leaving the real person with a ruined credit score and mystery medical debt.
</Callout>

## Online identity methods (techniques criminals use)

These are the specific digital tricks used to build fake personas or impersonate real people online.

- **Spoofing (IP, email, domain)** — forging headers/details so a message looks trusted. Includes **typosquatting** — registering look-alike domains like `panypal.com` instead of `paypal.com`.
- **Synthetic identity theft** — blending **real** stolen data with **fake** data to create a brand-new identity that belongs to no single real person, making it very hard for fraud systems to detect.
- **Social-media cloning** — scraping public photos and bio to build a parallel fake account that scams the victim's friends and family.
- **Malware / spyware, fake apps, data breaches, social engineering, and password-reuse attacks** all feed identity theft.

<Callout type="tip">
**Example (synthetic identity):** a criminal combines a stolen child's unused SSN with a fake name and address, slowly builds a great credit score over years with small cards, then disappears after taking a huge loan ("bust-out" fraud).
</Callout>

### Prevention

Never share OTP/PIN/password · check website addresses carefully · use **two-factor authentication** · avoid unknown links · keep software/antivirus updated · use strong, **unique** passwords per site.

<Callout type="exam">
**One-line answers**

- **Phishing:** tricking people into revealing confidential data by pretending to be trustworthy.
- **Spear phishing / whaling:** targeted phishing — whaling aims at top executives.
- **Identity theft:** illegal use of another person's PII for fraud or impersonation.
- **Synthetic identity:** a fake identity built from a mix of real and fabricated data.
- **Typosquatting:** registering look-alike domain names to trap users.
</Callout>
